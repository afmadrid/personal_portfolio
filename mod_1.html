Launching into Cyber Security

Unit 1

Cybersecurity is a topic with high relevance for companies. Many of them have learned the hard way by being attacked or they just want to prevent their resources and the integrity of their data. From 2018 to 2019 a reduction of companies affected by cybercrime was registered, decreasing from 43% to 32%. Nevertheless, the number of attacks in the remaining percentage increased. Hackers may be pointing to become more specialized and target specific objectives (Department for Digital, Culture, Media and Sport, 2019).

With the arriving of the COVID-19 pandemic cybercrime has used people necessity and altered state of mind as a window of opportunity. Because people under a situation that generates big stress and anxiety are more susceptible for cyberattacks such as phishing (Singh, Shepherd, Nurse, Erola, Epiphaniou, Maple, Bellekens, 2021). This helps to confirm that cybersecurity not only has a technological context, but also psychological and it not only affects companies but also individuals (Anderson, 2008).

There are several types of attacks, the most common ones are: virus, malware, phishing, denegation of service attacks (DOS) and advance persistent threats (APT) (VanSyckel, 2018). The APT attacks are the most dangerous because hackers generally exploit zero-day vulnerabilities and they infiltrate for a long period of time to understand better their targets (Khalid, Zainal, Aizaini, Ghaleb, 2021).

From a secure design perspective, it is recommendable to use a secure design approach for identifying threats and being prepared to be protected. Next principles must be considered when designing a secure software: stablish the context, develop systems as much difficult to infiltrate and disrupt, ensure that compromise is easy to detect and reduce the impact of the compromise (National Cyber Security Centre ,2019). It is recommended too that the secure approach is kept through the entire software development life cycle.
